About Inglewood

Location: Inglewood is situated in the southwestern part of Los Angeles County, bordered by several other cities, including Los Angeles, Hawthorne, and Culver City.

History: The area that is now Inglewood was originally inhabited by the Tongva Native American tribe before European settlers arrived in the late 18th century. Inglewood was incorporated as a city in 1908.

Diversity: Inglewood is known for its diverse population, with significant African American, Hispanic/Latino, White, Asian, and other ethnic communities contributing to its cultural fabric.

Community and Culture: Inglewood has a rich cultural scene, with various community events, festivals, and cultural celebrations throughout the year. The city is also home to several parks, recreational facilities, and cultural institutions that contribute to the quality of life for residents.

Transportation: The city is served by several major highways, including Interstate 405 and Interstate 105, providing convenient access to other parts of the Los Angeles metropolitan area. Public transportation options include bus services operated by the Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority (Metro).
